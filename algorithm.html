Using computational methods to identify genes that change during colon cancer.

Our Dataset

We analyzed an Affymetrix microarray dataset containing gene expression measurements from healthy colon tissue and colon cancer samples.

Healthy Samples

Represent normal colon tissue and provide a baseline for gene activity.

Cancer Samples

Represent tumor tissue where gene regulation may have changed.

Thousands of Genes

Each sample contains expression values for thousands of genes.

Differential Expression Analysis

Differential Gene Expression

The goal of differential expression analysis is to determine which genes are expressed differently between two groups.

Fold Change

Measures how much gene expression increases or decreases between samples.

p-value

Shows whether the observed difference is likely due to a real biological change.

Adjusted p-value

Corrects for testing thousands of genes at the same time.

Statistical Method: Limma

Limma (Linear Models for Microarray Data) uses statistical models to compare gene expression between groups.

How Limma Works

For each gene, Limma compares expression levels between healthy and cancer samples and calculates whether the difference is statistically significant.

Limma is useful because it can analyze thousands of genes while controlling for false discoveries.

Statistical Method: Welch's t-test

Welch's t-test compares the average gene expression between two groups while allowing the groups to have different variability.

Why Use It?

It provides another method for identifying genes that may differ between healthy and cancer samples.
